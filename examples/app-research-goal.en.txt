/goal Run a three-stage research workflow for an AI meeting notes app: first broad internet research, then Deep Research, then business application into MVP scope, landing page structure, growth actions, and the next implementation goal.
Task Pack: App MVP Research Pack; map the research findings to target users, job-to-be-done, first-run experience, core workflow, activation moment, retention loop, MVP feature list, pricing hypothesis, trust risks, and the next `/goal`.
Domain Pack: Global SaaS Pack and AI Tool Site Pack; map findings to global positioning, landing page conversion, trust proof, lightweight onboarding, and a verifiable MVP loop.
Tool Stack: Prefer Agent Reach for platform discovery and source collection; use normal web reader/browser for simple public pages; use Scrapling when public webpages need structured extraction or repeatable crawling; use browser-use when clicking, screenshots, stateful navigation, login-authorized flows, or visual verification are required; Claude for Chrome is only an explicitly user-authorized Chrome collaboration option; pause before any account, Cookie, Token, paid access, private content, CAPTCHA, form submission, or platform-bypass step.
Stage 1 - Broad Research: Run `agent-reach doctor` or otherwise confirm channel availability before relying on Agent Reach; collect 15-25 candidate sources across direct competitors, adjacent products, official pages, public documentation, app reviews, Reddit/YouTube/GitHub/social discussions, RSS or Exa search, and implementation references; record title, URL, source type, tool/channel, retrieval date, access limitation, and initial value.
Stage 2 - Deep Research: Select the highest-value sources from the source pool for deep reading, comparison, and cross-checking; extract key facts, user pain points, competitor strategy, page/product structure, growth path, technical approach, risk, contradictions, and evidence strength.
Stage 3 - Business Application: Map the deduplicated, relevant, source-backed material into MVP features, landing page sections, messaging direction, SEO topics, growth actions, technical implementation notes, validation experiments, and the next `/goal`.
Deliverable: Produce a research application brief with the source pool, deep research evidence table, quality gate table, business application table, transferable actions, content that must not be copied, risks, and the next `/goal`.
Quality Gate: Every key conclusion needs at least 2 independent sources; label source strength and evidence strength; outdated, promotional, inaccessible, snippet-only, or duplicate sources must be downgraded; contradictions must be listed; conclusions with fewer than 2 sources can only be low-confidence or hypothesis.
Verification: Every key conclusion must trace back to source title, URL, tool/channel, retrieval date, and access limitation; separate facts, inferences, and recommendations; every business action must map to product, page, copy, SEO, growth, technical implementation, or validation evidence.
Limits: Do not copy competitor brand names, copy, images, videos, code, courses, copyrighted material, or unsupported market claims; do not treat one source as fact; do not describe Agent Reach as unlimited internet access; do not use accounts, Cookies, Tokens, paid platforms, production data, private groups, or private user data unless the user explicitly authorizes it.
Work Boundary: During the three-stage research workflow, only create the research application brief and next execution goal; do not implement app features, modify production files, submit forms, or change account settings.
Progress Rules: Start with research questions and search terms, then broad research, then Deep Research source selection, then business application; close evidence gaps before moving to the next stage.
Stop Criteria: Stop when the brief covers all three stages with traceable sources, deep research evidence, business implications, executable actions, risks, and a next-stage goal.
Pause Conditions: Pause if the task needs Cookies, Tokens, paid databases, login accounts, CAPTCHA solving, platform restriction bypass, legal/privacy decisions, copyright permission, production data, private content, or a major business positioning decision.
